## The salary sacrifice secret

Salary sacrifice is one of the most valuable — and most misunderstood — benefits available to UK employees. When done correctly, it lets you put more money into your pension while your take-home pay falls by significantly less than the amount you're contributing.

## How it works

Under salary sacrifice, you agree with your employer to reduce your contractual gross salary by the amount you want to contribute to your pension. Your employer then pays that amount directly into your pension on your behalf.

The key word is *gross*. Because the sacrifice happens before tax and National Insurance are calculated, you save on both.

## The numbers

Suppose your salary is £50,000 and you want to contribute 5% (£2,500) to your pension.

**Without salary sacrifice (Relief at Source):**
- You contribute £2,000 (your 80%)
- HMRC adds £500 (basic rate relief)
- Pension receives £2,500
- Your take-home falls by approximately £1,600 (£2,000 minus tax relief you get back)

**With salary sacrifice:**
- Your gross salary reduces from £50,000 to £47,500
- Tax is calculated on £47,500 — saving ~£500 in Income Tax
- NI is calculated on £47,500 — saving ~£190 in National Insurance
- Your take-home falls by approximately £1,310
- The pension still receives £2,500

The same £2,500 into your pension costs you £190 less out of your pay packet.

At higher incomes — especially in the Personal Allowance taper zone — the saving is even more dramatic.

## What salary sacrifice cannot do

- It cannot reduce your salary below the National Living Wage
- It doesn't work if your employer doesn't offer it
- Some mortgage lenders look at your contractual salary (reduced by sacrifice), which can affect borrowing capacity
